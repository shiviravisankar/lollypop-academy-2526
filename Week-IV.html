Interview Tips for Quality Insights

üìù Before the Interview

  • Test your equipment: Make sure recording devices work
  • Prepare your environment: Find a quiet space without distractions
  • Review your questions: Be familiar with the flow without reading word-for-word
  • Prepare a brief script: Introduce yourself and explain the purpose of the interview

üó£Ô∏è During the Interview

  • Build rapport: Start with easy questions to make participants comfortable
  • Use silence effectively: Give users time to think and elaborate
  • Stay focused but flexible: Follow interesting tangents while keeping on track
  • Take notes on non-verbal cues: Observe frustration, confusion, or excitement

‚úÖ After the Interview

  • Debrief immediately: Write down key insights while fresh in your mind
  • Transcribe important quotes: Capture exact wording for powerful insights
  • Review recordings: Listen for things you might have missed
  • Identify initial patterns: Note similarities across different interviews
