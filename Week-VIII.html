1. Understand & Align with Stakeholder Priorities

Tailor your presentation to stakeholders' interests and knowledge levels. Focus on insights that directly impact their concerns and decision-making.

2. Make it Actionable

Highlight 1‚Äì3 key recommendations early. Support each with concise data, user quotes, and context that connects to business objectives.

3. Enhance Comprehension Through Storytelling

4. Visualize the Story

Use visual aids like journey maps, charts, and infographics to simplify complex information. Ensure visuals are clear and purposeful.

6. Drive Actionable Outcomes

Structure the presentation to bridge the gap between user research and business decision-making. The goal is informed action, not just information.

üó∫Ô∏è User Journey Mapping

(A Tool for Stakeholder Engagement)

A user journey map is a visual representation of the steps a user takes to achieve a goal, capturing their actions, thoughts, and emotions throughout the process. It's one of the most effective ways to communicate user research to stakeholders.

Key Components

  • Persona: User profile (demographics, behaviors)
  • Scenario: Specific task or situation
  • Phases: Stages of the journey
  • Actions & Emotions: What users do and feel
  • Pain Points: Challenges users face
  • Opportunities: Areas for improvement

Why It's Effective

  • Visualizes the entire user experience
  • Aligns cross-functional teams around user needs
  • Highlights opportunities for improvement
  • Facilitates empathy and user-centric thinking
  • Drives strategic decision-making
  • Connects research insights to business outcomes

Best Practices

  • Base it on real research, not assumptions
  • Keep it visually simple but informative
  • Highlight emotions throughout the journey
  • Make pain points and opportunities clear
  • Customize for your stakeholder audience
  • Use it as a discussion tool, not just a deliverable

üìä Structuring Your Stakeholder Presentation

(From Research to Recommendations)

A well-structured presentation guides stakeholders from understanding the problem to embracing your solutions. Here's a framework that works well for most UX research and design presentations.

1. Executive Summary

Start with 1-3 key findings and recommendations. This is for stakeholders who may only stay for the beginning.

2. Research Context

Briefly explain what you researched, why, and how. Keep methodology brief unless specifically asked.

3. Key Insights

Present 3-5 major insights from research. Support each with evidence: quotes, statistics, observations.

4. User Journey Map

Visualize the current user experience, highlighting pain points and emotional highs/lows.

5. Design Solutions

Show how your wireframes address specific pain points identified in research and journey mapping.

6. Business Impact

Connect your recommendations to business metrics: conversion, retention, satisfaction, or efficiency gains.

7. Next Steps & Timeline

Propose concrete actions, ownership, and timeline. Be specific about what you need from stakeholders.

8. Q&A Discussion

Allow ample time for questions and discussion. This is often where the most valuable conversations happen.

‚úÖ Presentation Best Practices

(Do's and Don'ts for Stakeholder Engagement)

DO

  • Know your audience and their priorities
  • Lead with recommendations, not just findings
  • Use real user quotes and specific examples
  • Connect insights to business metrics
  • Use visuals to simplify complex information
  • Practice your delivery multiple times
  • Anticipate questions and prepare answers

DON'T

  • Overwhelm with research methodology details
  • Present findings without recommendations
  • Use technical UX jargon unnecessarily
  • Show every piece of data you collected
  • Criticize current solutions without alternatives
  • Leave next steps ambiguous
  • Run over your allocated time

"The goal isn't to show how much work you did‚Äîit's to drive decisions and actions that improve the user experience and business outcomes."

  • Focus on the "so what" of your findings, not just the "what"
  • User journey maps are powerful tools for communicating the user experience
  • Structure your presentation to guide stakeholders from problems to solutions
  • Connect your recommendations to business metrics and outcomes
