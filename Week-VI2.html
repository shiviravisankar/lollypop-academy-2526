üí° What is Competitor Analysis?

(Learning from existing solutions in the marketplace)

Competitor analysis is the process of studying companies that offer similar products or services to understand their strengths, weaknesses, user experience patterns, and business strategies. It helps us identify industry standards, understand user expectations, spot opportunities to differentiate, and learn from others' successes and mistakes.

"It's not just about what your competitors are doing ‚Äî it's about why they're doing it."

üîç Why Do We Analyze Competitors?

(Finding opportunities by understanding the landscape)

To Learn From What Works (And What Doesn't)

Save time by building on proven solutions rather than reinventing the wheel.

To Uncover Gaps We Can Uniquely Fill

Identify opportunities in the market that competitors aren't addressing.

To Build Better Products By Design, Not Just Instinct

Make informed decisions based on evidence rather than assumptions.

To Help Align Business + User Expectations

Create realistic benchmarks that balance innovation with familiarity.

üìä What is Benchmarking?

(Comparing specific features and metrics)

Benchmarking is the act of comparing specific features, flows, or metrics from different competitors to evaluate how your product stacks up. You can benchmark things like onboarding time, number of steps in a checkout flow, visual consistency or loading speed, language tone and clarity, and conversion rates (if data is available).

Dimension What to Look For
Onboarding Clarity, steps, friction points
Navigation Menus, filters, search
Visual Design Consistency, accessibility, branding
Language & Tone Friendly, formal, localised?
Microinteractions Feedback on actions? Delight moments?
Conversion Path Checkout / booking / contact ease
Performance Page speed, responsiveness
Accessibility Color contrast, keyboard nav
