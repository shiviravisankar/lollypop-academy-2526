üß† Why is Creating a Portfolio a Form of UX?

Creating your portfolio is UX because:

  • You're thinking about user flows (what to show, in what order)

  • You do user research: Who will view this? A recruiter? A design lead?
  • You iterate: portfolios are never "done"

üßë‚Äçüíº Resume ‚Äì Your Invite to the Party

üéØ What's its goal?
To get you that first call or interview. That's it.
No one is reading it to admire your choice of font. They're skimming for relevance.

‚ùå What It Shouldn't Look Like:

  • Overdesigned, unreadable layouts
  • A wall of text
  • 10-point font squeezed into 2 pages
  • Buzzword soup: "Synergizing user-centric paradigms"

‚úÖ What It Should Look Like:

  • Clean, structured, and scannable in under 10 seconds
  • Prioritized content (most relevant stuff on top)
  • Short, clear bullet points using Action + What + Tool + Outcome
    E.g., "Created wireframes using Figma for food delivery app in 2-week sprint"
  • Proof of potential, not proof of experience

üìå Best Practices for Entry-Level Designers:

  • Lead with projects: academic, freelance, bootcamp ‚Äî all count
  • Use clear role labels: UX Designer, UI Designer, etc.
  • Highlight process: research, testing, iteration ‚Äî even if informal
  • Avoid fluff: Only include things that directly support your goal
