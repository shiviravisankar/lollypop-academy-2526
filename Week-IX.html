‚úÖ "Wrong password. Try again."

Brevity

Description: Be concise without losing meaning

‚ùå "Invalid input"

‚úÖ "Enter a valid email address"

Especially in error pages, cancellations, and delays

üì± Microcopy Types & Best Practices

(The Building Blocks of User Experience)

Buttons

Purpose: Prompt action with clarity and urgency

Best Practices: Use verbs, be specific, avoid jargon

‚úÖ "Upload resume"

‚ùå "Click here"

Call-to-Actions

Purpose: Drive key tasks or conversions

Best Practices: Benefit-driven, short, consistent

‚úÖ "Start free trial"

‚úÖ "Find your ride"

Error Messages

Purpose: Inform and guide users through errors

Best Practices: Be clear, non-blaming, solution-oriented

‚úÖ "Your password must be at least 8 characters"

Empty States

Purpose: Clarify what's missing and suggest next steps

Best Practices: Comforting tone, helpful CTA

‚úÖ "You haven't added any items yet. Start browsing."

Tooltips

Purpose: Explain UI elements or functionality

Best Practices: Short, contextual, visible only when needed

‚úÖ "Add tags to help organize your notes"

Toasts / Confirmations

Purpose: Confirm user actions briefly

Best Practices: Friendly tone, temporary messages

‚úÖ "Saved successfully"

‚úÖ "Payment received"

Onboarding Steps

Purpose: Guide first-time users, reduce drop-off

Best Practices: Clear steps, visual cues, concise microcopy

‚úÖ "Step 1 of 3: Add your profile photo"
